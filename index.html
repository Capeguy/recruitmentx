Marketing

Marketing Cell supports the various NUS Students’ Computing Club events by running activities to fund the Club. These activities include working together with sponsors, handling merchandise sales, as well as planning and executing various fund-raising events such as our annual Bazaar, and Valentine’s Day.

Members of Marketing Cell will have opportunities to:
  • Build their network (including in the startup scene as I am looking to work more closely with startups this year)
  • Improve their communication skills (both written and verbal)
  • Work closely with the other Cells to implement up-and-coming projects, such as implementation of a website to handle merchandise sales
Roles Available:
Subcommittee Member
  • Outreach to sponsors (email, follow up)
  • Update database of potential sponsors
  • Support project directors / in-charges / fellow subcommittee members when necessary.
  • Help out with merchandise sales during camps / events
Project Director, Valentine’s Day
  • In charge of planning event timeline and activities
  • Source for vendors / sponsors for Valentine’s Day
  • Main contact point of sponsors / vendors for Valentine’s Day
  • Oversee the execution of the Valentine’s Day event
Project Director, Bazaar
  • In charge of planning event timeline and activities
  • Main contact point of sponsors / vendors for 2017’s Bazaar
  • Source for vendors for the Bazaar
  • Oversee the execution of the Bazaar
Merchandise Team
  • Oversee merchandise sales during camps / events
  • Work closely with Publicity to generate new ideas for merchandises such as holding contests etc.
  • Work closely with IT to oversee implementation and execute web-store to sell merchandise

Academic Liaison
Academic Cell aims to provide a holistic academic environment for SOC students. These will include, but are not limited to:
  1. Helping ensure that students know the academic opportunities available to them, in terms of talks, internships, seminars, etc.
  2. Ensuring that students have the resources necessary to augment their studies, such as crash courses, tuition and mock exams.
  3. Serve as a point of contact with UG office, allowing students to voice out concerns regarding module planning, course curriculum, and bringing it to the attention of the UG office.

Roles Available:
Planning Team
These people will be in charge of helping to organize and manage curriculum for the courses, mock exams, and intro workshops. Ideally, will require basic knowledge in Java, Python, C, JS, R. Not well versed in all the languages, but at least know the basics for about 2 to 3. This core team will also help in planning of the various milestones.

Subcomm member
These people not only serve as eyes and ears to actively collect feedback on the student body’s academic needs and feedback it to us, they will also help to run the various events. This will also become an opportunity for them to expand their knowledge and pick up new skills.

Responsibilities:
  • Acad Day
  • Mock PE
  • Java Confidence Course + others if necessary
  • Intro workshops (Tentative)
  • Hackathon (Tentative)
Sports

The sports cell is responsible for several events throughout the year, the highlight being IFG. This year our aim is to recruit members who are passionate about creating a sporting environment here in SoC. Some events in which we will be undertaking would be Sports camp, IFG, possibly Sports week bi-weekly sports games.

A Sport’s Comm member should be willing to give up time (especially on the weekends during IFG period) to come down and support your fellow faculty peers as they compete in their respective IFG games. Through your time in the Sport’s Comm I wish you’ll not only be able to develop your character but also make the MC part of your family.

I hope that through the 19th MC’s Sport’s cell, we will be able to invoke a positive change in SoC’s sports culture.

Roles Available:
Sports camp PD
  • Responsible for the overseeing and the run through of sports camp (at the end of the year)
  • Needs the passion to organize and run a camp
Sub-comm members
  • Willing to sacrifice time and even weekends during busy periods
  • Organize as well as to help out in Sports camp/ IFG management/ Sports week
  • To be able to be depended on when the time calls for it
  • Rotation of bi-weekly sports IC role, where comm members will take turns organizing a ‘mini’ sports game for SoC students
  • Able to communicate and work well with others (mainly for Captain/Team manager recruitment purposes)
Welfare

The welfare cell is a group of friends who come together to bring others together. We aim for SoC students to feel like they are part of a community here, and we want to enrich the lives of students. We are responsible for giving them more opportunities to enjoy student life through various events that we organise. Welfare subcommittee members will have the opportunity to propose ideas to better the lives of fellow students, as well as take part in the execution of those ideas.

Roles Available:
Core team member
Core team members will be involved planning process of events, as well as have leadership opportunities to take charge of the events. They have a direct say in the actual gameplan and will be the ones who carry out their own plans.

Cell member
Cell members have a lower level of commitment as compared to core team members. They will provide the hands and legs on the ground for events, ensuring that each event runs seamlessly. Examples of responsibilities: station masters for events, packing of exam welfare packs etc.

Freshmen Orientation Projects

Freshmen Orientation Projects cell’s main responsibility is in planning orientation events for freshmen who will be joining the School of Computing family. Our projects are the first opportunities to help freshmen transit into university life and immerse themselves into the faculty. Our vision of ONE FOP hopes to provide freshmen with a smooth and harmonised orientation experience throughout the whole summer.

As a FOP subcommittee member, you will have the opportunity to express your creativity and resourcefulness in planning for the various orientation camps and activities. Not only that, you will be a senior and a friend that the incoming freshman will look upon to whenever they are in need. Besides all of these, being part of FOP also means you will have the chance to meet you fellow batchmates and forge new friendships with one another which may last beyond your life in NUS.

Roles Available:
Freshmen Social Camp Project Director/Committee
  • Plan programs and games for incoming freshmen
  • Encourage socialising within freshmen
  • Recruit Group Leaders, Helpers and Househeads
  • Work with RAG team to recruit RAGgers
Freshmen Orientation Camp Project Director/Committee
  • Plan programs and games for incoming freshmen
  • Help freshmen be familiarised with school’s facilities and vicinity
  • Help freshmen integrate into school life
  • Recruit Group Leaders, Helpers, Househeads
  • Work with Flag team to recruit Flaggers
RAG Project Director/Committee
  • Coordinate RAG performance
  • Choreograph RAG dance
  • Plan and build float
  • Source for costume/float materials
Flag Project Director/Committee
  • Plan fundraisers for beneficiary
  • Arrange annual flag day
  • Plan programs for SoC beneficiary interaction
Bash Project Director/Committee
  • Plan Bash night program
  • Acquire Venue
  • Hire necessary staff (DJ, make-up artist, photographer etc)
  • Arrange Bash photoshoot
  • Work with FSC and FOW to plan finale night
  • Pageant Selection

Logistics

Logistics Cell aims to support all the NUS Students’ Computing Club related events to the best of our ability. Besides, this 19th Logistics Cell should uphold the spirit of all-for-one, one-for-all kind of mentality so as to bond well and eventually be able to work well together by harnessing one another’s strengths and weaknesses.

Next, as members of the Logistics cell, we should also take note and raise any cleanliness issue (Computing Club and Student Lounge) as well as furniture damage (tables, chairs and etc) because we are students as well and we definitely want the best for our student body.

Lastly, I hope that every member in the Logistics Cell would have the chance to learn to take up certain responsibilities such as being the “In-charge” for certain roles. The roles would be rotational monthly and this would allow members of Logistics Cell to not only know how to work hard, but also learn something out of it.

Roles Available:
Procurement IC
  • To purchase miscellaneous items that Computing Club needs or items that certain Computing Club event requires (e.g. thumbtack, masking tape, markers and etc).
Cleanliness IC
  • To ensure cleanliness of Computing Club Room and Student Lounge.
  • Come up with cleaning roster for Logistics Cell members. (Anyone that did not do their job when it’s their turn, then notify the Deputy/Director)
Sign In/Out IC
  • To ensure that items that are signed out comes back
  • In the event that items are returned but the borrower forget to sign in, contact them and get them to sign
Other Subcommittee members (including the 3 ICs stated above)
  • To help out in cleaning/re-arranging of storeroom (monthly)
  • You are encourage to join planning committee of other cell’s events (so that you learn more things)
  • In the event of manpower shortage for Computing Club events, subcommittee members are encouraged to help out (e.g. welfare packing and etc)

*Take note: The 3ICs will change every month, so every subcommittee members have a chance to take up different responsibilities*
